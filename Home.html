A Little History

Desmond Thomas Doss (February 7, 1919 â€“ March 23, 2006) was a United States Army Corporal who served as a combat medic with an infantry company in World War II. After distinguishing himself in the Battle of Okinawa, he became the first conscientious objector to receive the Medal of Honor for actions above and beyond the call of duty. He is also the only conscientious objector to receive the medal during World War II.

Desmond Doss was the Hero of the second war, not because he kills the enemyes, but because he saved your partners whithout take a gun.


Awards

Here's a list of the awards received:

  • Asiatic-Pacific Campaign Medal

  • Philippine Liberation Medal
  • Distinguished Unit Citation
  • Meritorious Unit Commendation
